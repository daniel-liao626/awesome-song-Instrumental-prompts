## Song Structure Basics 
Every song is built from a few common sections:

**Core (required)**

-   **Verse**: The **main part** of the song. Tells the story, develops the idea, provides details.
-   **Chorus (Hook)**: The **most memorable** part. Repeats the same lyrics/melody, usually the "catchy" highlight.

**Optional (use as needed)**

-   **Pre-Chorus**: Connects the verse to the chorus, builds anticipation.
-   **Bridge**: Provides contrast, new perspective, or tension before the final chorus.
-   **Intro**: Sets the mood and key at the beginning (instrumental or short lyrical).
-   **Outro**: Closes the song, often repeating phrases or fading out.
-   **Solo**: Instrumental or vocal solo section.

## Tempo Guide for AI Music Creation

Understanding tempo is crucial for AI music generation. Use these tempo ranges and descriptions to create music with the perfect pace and energy.

### Lento to Largo (Very Slow)

>Grave: 20-40 BPM,
>Largo: 40-60 BPM,
>Larghetto: 60-66 BPM

### Adagio (Slow)

>Lento: 45-60 BPM,
>Adagissimo: Very slow,
>Adagio: 66-76 BPM

### Moderato (Moderate)

>Moderato: 108-120 BPM,
>Allegretto: 112-120 BPM,
>Tempo comodo: At a comfortable pace

### Allegro (Fast)

>Allegro: 120-168 BPM,
>Vivace: 140-176 BPM,
>Molto allegro: Very fast

### Presto to Prestissimo (Very Fast)

>Presto: 168-200 BPM,
>Prestissimo: 200+ BPM,
>Allegrissimo: 172-176 BPM

## Best Practices for Prompting Music
The heart of AI music generation lies in the **prompt**. This is the textual instruction you provide to AI, outlining your desired musical style, instrumentation, tempo, and mood. The more descriptive you are, the better the AI can understand and respond.
Think of all the different ways you can describe a song: genre, mood, atmosphere, dynamics, instruments, tempo. These descriptors shape the output.
ðŸ‘‰ Avoid vague terms like "good"or "interesting."Use rich, descriptive words like "ethereal" ,"dreamlike" ,"nostalgic",or "minimalist".

**Key Takeaways**
-   Start with **genre + style** (e.g., "synth-pop," "jazz," "cinematic").
-   Add **instruments** and **textures** (e.g., "jangly guitars," "sweeping strings").
-   Define **tempo** (slow, mid-tempo, fast, very fast).
-   Specify **mood/atmosphere** (nostalgic, dreamy, ominous, playful).
-   Include **details** for extra nuance (e.g., "walking bassline," "epic climax").
